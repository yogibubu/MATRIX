## PI Narrative To Build

The PI case should not read as a software-development CV. It should establish
scientific leadership in:

- theoretical and computational spectroscopy;
- quantum chemistry and molecular structure;
- development of chemically interpretable models;
- integration of methods into tools used by the community;
- successful supervision and international collaborations;
- ability to deliver long-term high-risk methodology.
- ability to coordinate broad projects where theory, algorithms, software,
  validation, applications and experiment interact continuously.

The central PI message for MATRIX is that the breadth of the project is not a
liability because the PI's CV already demonstrates the ability to keep many
scientific layers under control at the same time. The narrative should show a
career pattern: theoretical developments are converted into robust
computational tools, then tested against experimental observables and used to
refine the theory.

Retirement should be framed as scientific focus, not as disengagement. The CV
and publication statistics show that, after retirement in 2023, the PI's
scientific activity has remained very high and has arguably become more
concentrated on new ideas because administrative duties no longer compete with
research. This is directly relevant to MATRIX: the project needs sustained
conceptual leadership more than routine administrative visibility.

## PI Leadership Argument

Use this argument explicitly in B1:

> MATRIX is deliberately broad, but it is not diffuse. Its breadth mirrors the
> PI's established way of working: theory, implementation, validation and
> experiment are developed together, so that methodological advances remain
> anchored to measurable molecular observables.

Add this complementary argument:

> The PI's retirement has not reduced scientific activity; it has removed
> administrative distractions. The most recent publication record and the
> emergence of PCS, NanoLEGO/synthons, accuracy-ladder and rotational-refinement
> ideas show a phase of concentrated scientific creativity directly aligned
> with MATRIX.

Evidence to insert:

- landmark papers where theoretical spectroscopy was validated by experiment;
- semiexperimental or empirical-equilibrium-structure work connecting computed
  models to measured rotational/vibrational data;
- method-development papers that became reusable computational tools;
- collaborations with experimental groups and databases;
- examples where discrepancies with experiment led to improved theory or
  protocols;
- leadership of teams combining quantum chemistry, spectroscopy, coding and
  applications.
- post-retirement publications and concepts that demonstrate current momentum,
  not only past reputation.
