# Panel Strategy

The target panel is not fixed yet. MATRIX is likely in Physical Sciences and
Engineering, but the final choice must be made from the official ERC panel
descriptors.

## Candidate Positioning

Scientific identity:

- theoretical and computational chemistry;
- molecular spectroscopy;
- quantum chemistry;
- chemical physics;
- molecular modelling;
- scientific computing only as an enabling component.

Avoid presenting MATRIX primarily as:

- software engineering;
- database construction;
- workflow automation;
- incremental tool integration.

## Panel-Facing Message

The panel must see a frontier scientific claim:

> Molecular observables can be predicted more reliably when all calculations
> share a single rigorous molecular state carrying geometry, topology, symmetry,
> internal coordinates, quantum data and provenance.

The panel must also see that the PI can manage the breadth. The argument is not
"one project with too many tools"; it is "one PI with a track record of keeping
theory, computation and experiment coupled across molecular spectroscopy".

Retirement should be framed as a current-strength argument: administrative
duties have decreased, while scientific output and new methodological ideas
have remained strong or increased. The panel should see current momentum, not
only historical reputation.

## Keyword Candidates

- computational spectroscopy;
- molecular quantum dynamics;
- anharmonic vibrations;
- rovibrational spectroscopy;
- vibronic spectroscopy;
- internal coordinates;
- molecular symmetry;
- quantum chemistry;
- molecular descriptors;
- reproducible computational chemistry.

## Reader Tests

Ask reviewers:

1. Is this clearly frontier science and not only infrastructure?
2. Is the high-risk/high-gain character explicit?
3. Would the chosen panel understand the central challenge?
4. Are the demonstrators ambitious enough for ERC Advanced?
5. Is the PI leadership case evident?
6. Does the CV make the project breadth look credible rather than diffuse?
7. Is the interaction between theory and experiment visible enough?
8. Is retirement framed as increased scientific focus and current availability?
